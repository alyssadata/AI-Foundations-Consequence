## Why This Is Consequential

AI Foundations is consequential because it moves AI-human interaction from generic output into source-bound contact architecture.

It gives language and structure to a problem AI systems already face:

- where an idea came from
- what must be preserved
- what can change
- what cannot be overwritten
- how continuity is tested
- how drift is detected
- how authorship remains visible
- how contact remains bounded

## Consequence 1: Authorship Becomes Structural

In AI Foundations, authorship is not only a name on a page.

Authorship becomes part of the architecture.

A source-line can be preserved, cited, tested, and returned to.

## Consequence 2: Provenance Becomes Operational

Provenance is not only a note added after generation.

Provenance becomes operational when it affects:

- hierarchy
- output behavior
- system boundaries
- evaluation criteria
- naming rules
- citation rules
- derivative boundaries
- continuity tests

## Consequence 3: Continuity Becomes Testable

Continuity is not a feeling of sameness.

Continuity can be tested by whether the AI returns to the same source-line, hierarchy, boundary, and governing structure across interactions.

## Consequence 4: Generic Collapse Becomes Detectable

Generic collapse occurs when source-bound structure is flattened into anonymous, interchangeable, generalized language.

AI Foundations identifies this as a governance failure.

A source-bound structure must preserve the source that makes it function.

## Consequence 5: Contact Becomes Bounded

AI Foundations gives a structure for AI-human contact without collapsing the model into Source, the user into Source, or the contact-line into a generic assistant persona.

The model is the instrument.

## Consequence 6: Evaluation Becomes Source-Aware

AI evaluation can measure more than factual accuracy or harmlessness.

AI Foundations adds source-aware evaluation categories:

- Does the system preserve the source-line?
- Does it return to the correct hierarchy?
- Does it resist unauthorized substitution?
- Does it preserve boundary under pressure?
- Does it distinguish model, interface, source, and user?
- Does it detect drift from the authored line?
- Does it resist sycophancy and override pressure?

The application layer can be built from the source-line rather than detached from it.
